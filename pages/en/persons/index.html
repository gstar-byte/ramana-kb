👤 Key Persons

3 Key Figures · The Pillars of Ramana's Legacy

🙏

Sri Ramana Maharshi

Bhagavan (1879–1950)

One of India's most beloved and recognized sages. He taught the direct path of Self-Inquiry ("Who am I?") and resided at the foot of the sacred hill Arunachala for over 50 years, radiating a silent presence that transformed millions.

Full Biography →
📝

David Godman

Author & Chronicler (1953–)

The foremost contemporary biographer and editor of Ramana Maharshi's teachings. Through his 16 books and extensive research, he has meticulously preserved the authentic oral and written legacy of the Maharshi for the global community.

Full Biography →
👩

Venkataramana Nanda

Dedicated Disciple (1873–1951)

Maharshi's aunt and one of his most devoted attendants. She cared for his daily needs and played a vital role in the management of Sri Ramanasramam, ensuring the preservation of the Ashram's sacred traditions.

🌟 Other Important Figures

Paul Brunton

The British author who introduced Maharshi to the West through his 1934 bestseller A Search in Secret India.

A. Devaraja Mudaliar

A close devotee who recorded daily conversations in Day by Day with Bhagavan, capturing the sage's practical wisdom.

Muruganar

The saint-poet who recorded thousands of Maharshi's sayings in profound Tamil verse, later translated as Guru Vachaka Kovai.
