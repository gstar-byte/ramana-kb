## 二、精选问答（35对）

### 来自《Talks with Sri Ramana Maharshi》

**问答1：**
- **问：** How to realise that all the world is God?
- **答：** If you make your outlook that of wisdom, you will find the world to be God. Without knowing the Supreme Spirit (Brahman), how will you find His all-pervasiveness?
- **出处：** Talk 1, 15th May, 1935

**问答2：**
- **问：** What is the nature of happiness?
- **答：** If a man thinks that his happiness is due to external causes and his possessions, it is reasonable to conclude that his happiness must increase with the increase of possessions and diminish in proportion to their diminution. Therefore if he is devoid of possessions, his happiness should be nil. What is the real experience of man? Does it conform to this view? In deep sleep the man is devoid of possessions, including his own body. Instead of being unhappy he is quite happy. Everyone desires to sleep soundly. The conclusion is that happiness is inherent in man and is not due to external causes. One must realise his Self in order to open the store of unalloyed happiness.
- **出处：** Talk 3

**问答3：**
- **问：** Is a Master necessary for realisation?
- **答：** The realisation is the result of the Master's grace more than teachings, lectures, meditation, etc. They are only secondary aids, whereas the former is the primary and the essential cause.
- **出处：** Talk 13, 7th January, 1935

**问答4：**
- **问：** What are the obstacles which hinder realisation of the Self?
- **答：** They are habits of mind (vasanas).
- **出处：** Talk 13

**问答5：**
- **问：** How to overcome the mental habits (vasanas)?
- **答：** By realising the Self.
- **出处：** Talk 13

**问答6：**
- **问：** What are the aids for realisation?
- **答：** The teachings of the Scriptures and of realised souls.
- **出处：** Talk 13

**问答7：**
- **问：** How long will it take for one to get that?
- **答：** Why do you desire to know?
- **追问：** To give me hope.
- **答：** Even such a desire is an obstacle. The Self is ever there, there is nothing without it. Be the Self and the desires and doubts will disappear.
- **出处：** Talk 13

**问答8：**
- **问：** Should I remain as if in sleep and be watchful at the same time?
- **答：** Yes. Watchfulness is the waking state. Therefore the state will not be one of sleep, but sleepless sleep.
- **出处：** Talk 13

**问答9：**
- **问：** Does my realisation help others?
- **答：** Yes, certainly. It is the best help possible. But there are no others to be helped. For a realised being sees the Self, just like a goldsmith estimating the gold in various jewels. When you identify yourself with the body then only the forms and shapes are there. But when you transcend your body the others disappear along with your body-consciousness.
- **出处：** Talk 13

**问答10：**
- **问：** Can anyone get any benefit by repeating sacred syllables (mantras) picked up casually?
- **答：** No. He must be competent and initiated in such mantras.
- **出处：** Talk 8

### 来自《Maharshi's Gospel》

**问答11：**
- **问：** What is the highest goal of spiritual experience for man?
- **答：** Self-realization.
- **出处：** Book I, Chapter I

**问答12：**
- **问：** Can a married man realise the Self?
- **答：** Certainly. Married or unmarried, a man can realise the Self; because That is here and now. If it were not so, but attainable by some effort at some time, and if it were new and had to be acquired, it would not be worth pursuit. Because, what is not natural is not permanent either. But what I say is that the Self is here and now, and alone.
- **出处：** Book I, Chapter I

**问答13：**
- **问：** Should I not renounce my home?
- **答：** If that had been your destiny the question would not have arisen.
- **出处：** Book I, Chapter I

**问答14：**
- **问：** Is it possible to enjoy samadhi while busy in worldly work?
- **答：** The feeling 'I work' is the hindrance. Ask yourself 'who works?' Remember who you are. Then the work will not bind you; it will go on automatically.
- **出处：** Book I, Chapter I

**问答15：**
- **问：** Is a vow of silence useful?
- **答：** The inner silence is self-surrender. And that is living without the sense of ego.
- **出处：** Book I, Chapter II

**问答16：**
- **问：** Is solitude necessary for a sannyasin?
- **答：** Solitude is in the mind of a man. One might be in the thick of the world and yet maintain perfect serenity of mind; such a person is always in solitude. Another may stay in the forest, but still be unable to control his mind. He cannot be said to be in solitude.
- **出处：** Book I, Chapter II

**问答17：**
- **问：** How can I control the mind?
- **答：** There is no mind to control if the Self is realised. The Self shines forth when the mind vanishes. In the realised man the mind may be active or inactive, the Self alone exists.
- **出处：** Book I, Chapter III

**问答18：**
- **问：** If the mind is merely a shadow how then is one to know the Self?
- **答：** The Self is the heart, self-luminous. Illumination arises from the heart and reaches the brain, which is the seat of the mind. The world is seen with the mind; so you see the world by the reflected light of the Self.
- **出处：** Book I, Chapter III

**问答19：**
- **问：** Is a set meditation necessary for strengthening the mind?
- **答：** Not if you keep the idea always before you that it is not you who are doing the work but the Self.
- **出处：** Book I, Chapter III

**问答20：**
- **问：** What is mauna (silence)?
- **答：** That state which transcends speech and thought is mauna; it is meditation without mental activity. Subjugation of the mind is meditation; deep meditation is eternal speech.
- **出处：** Book I, Chapter II

### 来自《Day by Day with Bhagavan》

**问答21：**
- **问：** Should I give up my business and take to reading books on Vedanta?
- **答：** If the objects have an independent existence, i.e., if they exist anywhere apart from you, then it may be possible for you to go away from them. But they don't exist apart from you; they owe their existence to you, your thought. So, where can you go, to escape them? As for reading books on Vedanta, you may go on reading any number of them. They can only tell you, 'Realise the Self within you'. The Self cannot be found in books. You have to find it out for yourself, in yourself.
- **出处：** 16-3-45, Morning

**问答22：**
- **问：** It is said the world and the objects that we see are all unreal, like the snake in the rope. It is also stated in other places that the seer and the seen are the same. If the seer and the seen are same, then how can we say that the seen is unreal?
- **答：** All that is meant is that the seen regarded as an independent entity, independent of the Self, is unreal. The seen is not different from the seer. What exists is the one Self, not a seer and a seen. The seen regarded as the Self is real.
- **出处：** 19-3-45, Morning

**问答23：**
- **问：** It is said only some are chosen for Self-realisation and those alone could get it. It is rather discouraging.
- **答：** All that is meant is, we cannot by our own buddhi, unaided by God's grace, achieve realisation of Self.
- **出处：** 19-3-45, Morning

**问答24：**
- **问：** How to conquer passions?
- **答：** If the passions are something external to us we can take arms and ammunition and conquer them. They all come from within us. If, by looking into the source whence they issue, we see that they don't come out of us, we shall conquer them.
- **出处：** 31-3-45, Afternoon

**问答25：**
- **问：** Do these samskaras (tendencies) go gradually or will they suddenly disappear one day?
- **答：** When the sun rises, does the darkness go gradually or all at once?
- **出处：** 31-3-45, Afternoon

### 来自《Reflections on Talks》

**问答26：**
- **问：** How to avoid misery?
- **答：** Has misery a shape? Misery is only an unwanted thought. The mind is not strong enough to resist it. It can be strengthened by worship of God.
- **出处：** Chapter I, Section 1

**问答27：**
- **问：** I have no peace of mind. Something prevents it — probably my destiny.
- **答：** What is destiny? There is no destiny. Surrender and all will be well. Throw all the responsibility on God. Do not bear the burden yourself. What can destiny do to you then?
- **出处：** Chapter I, Section 2

**问答28：**
- **问：** May I have Guru's Grace?
- **答：** Grace is always there.
- **追问：** But I do not feel it.
- **答：** Surrender will make one understand Grace.
- **追问：** I have surrendered heart and soul. I am the best judge of my heart; still I do not feel the Grace.
- **答：** If you have surrendered the question would not have arisen.
- **出处：** Chapter I, Section 11

**问答29：**
- **问：** Is there a state beyond our efforts and effortlessness?
- **答：** Until it is realised, effort is necessary. After tasting such bliss even once, one will repeatedly try to regain it. Having once experienced the bliss of peace, no one would like to be out of it, or engage himself otherwise.
- **出处：** Chapter I, Section 6

**问答30：**
- **问：** What is happiness? Is it inherent in the Self or in the object, or in the contact between the subject and the object?
- **答：** When there is contact with a desirable object or memory thereof, and when there is freedom from undesirable contacts, or memory thereof, we say there is happiness. Such happiness is relative and is better called pleasure. But we want absolute and permanent happiness. This does not reside in objects but in the Absolute. It is peace free from pain and pleasure — it is a neutral state.
- **出处：** Chapter I, Section 5

- **出处：** Chapter XI

**问答32：**
- **问：** Is a Guru necessary for realisation?
- **答：** I have not said that a Guru is not necessary. But a Guru need not always be in human form.

**问答33：**
- **问：** It is asked, why all this creation so full of sorrow and evil.
- **答：** All one can say is that it is God's will, which is inscrutable. No motive, no desire, no end to achieve can be attributed to that infinite, all-wise and all-powerful Being.
- **出处：** Chapter V

**问答34：**
- **问：** To whom does maya come?
- **答：** To the mind or ego who feels that he is a separate entity, who thinks 'I do this' or 'This is mine'.
- **追问：** Where does it come from and how did it originate?
- **答：** Nobody can say.
- **追问：** How did it arise?
- **答：** Through non-vichara, through failure to enquire 'Who am I?'
- **出处：** Chapter II

**问答35：**
- **问：** What is the best way of killing the ego?
- **答：** To each person that way is best which appears easiest or appeals the most. All the ways are equally good, as they lead to the same goal, which is the merging of the ego in the Self. What the bhakta calls surrender, the man who does vichara calls jnana. Both are trying to take the ego back to the source from which it sprang and make it merge there.
- **出处：** Chapter IV

## 三、修行方法的具体指导（20条）

### 1. "我是谁"的参究法

**指导1：**
> "控制言语和呼吸，深入自己内心，就像一个人潜入水中寻找掉落的物品，必须找到自我升起的源头，通过敏锐的洞察力。"
> 
> ——《Gems from Bhagavan》, Chapter IV

**指导2：**
> "对于所有念头，源头是'我念'。心智只有通过'我是谁'的自我参究才会消融。'我是谁'的念头会摧毁所有其他念头，最终也会杀死自己。"
> 
> ——《Gems from Bhagavan》, Chapter IV

**指导3：**
> "当其他念头升起时，不要试图完成它们，必须参究这个念头是对谁升起的。每当一个念头升起，你必须警觉并问这个念头是对谁发生的。答案将是'对我'。如果你参究'我是谁'，心智将回到它的源头。"
> 
> ——《Gems from Bhagavan》, Chapter IV

**指导4：**
> "参究不是口头上重复'我'、'我'，而是通过深度内转的心智寻找'我'从哪里升起。"
> 
> ——《Gems from Bhagavan》, Chapter IV

### 2. 呼吸控制（Pranayama）

**指导5：**
> "呼吸控制可以作为辅助，但本身永远不能引导到目标。在做的时候，要在心智上保持警觉，记住'我念'和对源头的追寻。然后你会发现呼吸下沉的地方，'我念'就在那里升起。它们一起下沉和升起。"
> 
> ——《Gems from Bhagavan》, Chapter IV

**指导6：**
> "内部呼吸控制如下：Naham（我不是身体的观念）是呼气（rechaka），Koham（我是谁？）是吸气（puraka），Soham（我是他）是屏息（kumbhaka）。这样做，呼吸会自动得到控制。"
> 
> ——《Gems from Bhagavan》, Chapter III

**指导7：**
> "心智是骑手，呼吸是马。呼吸控制是对马的约束。通过那个约束，骑手也受到约束。"
> 
> ——《Gems from Bhagavan》, Chapter III

### 3. 臣服与归伏

**指导8：**
> "有两种实现臣服的方式。一种是看进'我'的源头并融入那个源头。另一种是感觉，'我自己是无助的，上帝是全能的，除了完全投身于祂，我没有其他安全的方法'；从而逐渐发展出只有上帝存在而自我不重要的信念。"
> 
> ——《Gems from Bhagavan》, Chapter V

**指导9：**
> "臣服于祂，按照祂的旨意安住，无论祂出现或消失；等待祂的喜悦。如果你要求祂按照你的喜好行事，那不是臣服而是对上帝发号施令。"
> 
> ——《Gems from Bhagavan》, Chapter V

**指导10：**
> "如果心智担心降临在我们身上的事情，或者我们犯下或忽略的事情，明智的做法是放弃责任感和自由意志，将自己视为全智和全能者的预定工具，按照祂的意愿去行和受苦。然后祂承担所有重担，给我们平安。"
> 
> ——《Gems from Bhagavan》, Chapter V

## 四、主要章节精华摘要

### 1. 《走向静默，如你本来》(Be As You Are)

**核心内容：**
本书是马哈希教导的入门经典，系统介绍了"我是谁"的参究方法。核心教导包括：
- 真我（Self）是唯一的实相，是一切存在的基础
- 幸福内在于真我，不依赖外在条件
- 通过持续的"我是谁"参究，可以消融心智和自我的幻象
- 臣服（surrender）与参究（enquiry）是通往实相的两扇门

**关键概念：**
- 真我（Self）：自照自明的意识
- 摩耶（Maya）：幻象的力量
- 参究（Vichara）：自我探究
- 臣服（Bhakti）：对神的奉献

### 2. 《日日与彼》(Day by Day with Bhagavan)

**核心内容：**
作者A. Devaraja Mudaliar记录了1942-1946年间在静修院与马哈希的日常接触。以日记形式记录了马哈希对访客问题的回答。

**精华摘要：**
- 世界和对象并非独立于观者存在，它们依赖于观者的思想
- 睡眠中的幸福证明幸福是内在的，不依赖外在拥有
- 参究"我是谁"会摧毁所有其他念头
- 命运（karma）可以通过臣服来超越
- 激情来自内在，通过寻找其源头可以征服它们

### 3. 《对谈录》(Talks with Sri Ramana Maharshi)

**核心内容：**
记录了1935-1939年间马哈希与访客和弟子的对话，由Munagala S. Venkataramiah记录。

**精华摘要：**
- 自我实现是灵性体验的最高目标
- 无论已婚或未婚，任何人都可以实现自我
- 工作是束缚还是解脱，取决于是否带着执着
- 孤独是心智的态度，不在于外在环境
- 控制心智的最好方法是实现真我

### 4. 《宝钻集》(Gems from Bhagavan)

**核心内容：**
由A. Devaraja Mudaliar编纂，按主题分类整理了马哈希的核心教导。

**章节精华：**
- **幸福章：** 幸福是自我的本性，与自我无异
- **自我与非我章：** 存在或意识是唯一的实相
- **心智章：** 心智是自我中升起的奇妙力量
- **参究章：** "我是谁"的参究是主要方法
- **臣服章：** 完全的臣服是智慧或解脱的另一个名字

### 5. 《马哈希福音》(Maharshi's Gospel)

**核心内容：**
分为两册，记录了马哈希对弟子问题的回答，涵盖工作与弃绝、静默与独处、心智控制、奉爱与智慧等主题。

**精华摘要：**
- **工作与弃绝：** 带着执着的工作是束缚，不带执着的工作不会影响做者
- **静默与独处：** 内在的静默是自我臣服，独处是心智的状态
- **心智控制：** 如果实现了真我，就没有心智需要控制
- **奉爱与智慧：** 奉爱是智慧之母，两者殊途同归

### 6. 《大瑜伽》(Maha Yoga)

**核心内容：**
由K. Lakshmana Sarma（笔名"WHO"）撰写，以吠檀多哲学阐释马哈希的教导。

**精华摘要：**
- 马哈希教导的核心是自我参究，这是所有瑜伽的总结
- 无知仅仅是将身体与自我认同
- 智者的状态是"自然状态"（Sahajabhava）
- 自我参究是"直接道路"，适合所有人

### 7. 《上师言颂》(Guru Vachaka Kovai)

**核心内容：**
由Muruganar记录，包含1284首诗，涵盖马哈希口头教导的方方面面。

**精华摘要：**
- 第一部分：真理的分析 - 世界的虚幻性、自我的本质
- 第二部分：真理的实践 - 各种修行方法的指导
- 第三部分：真理的体验 - 证悟者的状态描述
- 强调奉爱与智慧的不二性
- 详细描述了"我是"的参究方法

### 8. 《面对面》(Face to Face with Sri Ramana Maharshi)

**核心内容：**
收集了202位与马哈希有过直接接触的人的回忆录。

**精华摘要：**
- 马哈希的眼睛具有穿透力，能传递平安与喜悦
- 他的静默是最有力的教导
- 许多访客仅通过他的注视就获得灵性体验
- 他对所有生命形式都表现出平等的爱
- 他的存在本身就是一种祝福

## 六、核心教导总结

### 马哈希教导的精髓：

1. **真我（Self）是唯一实相**
   - 一切存在都是意识的显现
   - 真我是自照自明的，不需要被创造或获得

2. **"我是谁"的参究**
   - 持续追问"我是谁"，找到"我念"的源头
   - 当"我念"消融，真我就自然显现

3. **臣服（Surrender）**
   - 完全将自己交给神或上师
   - 臣服是智慧之母，两者殊途同归

4. **幸福是内在本性**
   - 不依赖外在条件或拥有物
   - 睡眠中的幸福证明了这一点

5. **静默是最有力的教导**
   - 马哈希主要通过静默教导
   - 言语会中断永恒的"语言"
